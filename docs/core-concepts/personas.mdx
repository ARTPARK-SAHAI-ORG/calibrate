# Personas

Personas define the characteristics of simulated users that interact with your agents during testing. They determine WHO the simulated user is and HOW they behave during conversations.

## Overview

Personas are used in:
- End-to-end simulations
- Testing agent responses with different user types
- Evaluating agent performance across demographics

---

## Creating a Persona

### Step 1: Navigate to Personas

From the sidebar, click **Personas** to view your existing personas.

### Step 2: Click "Add persona"

Click the **Add persona** button in the top right corner. A sidebar panel will open.

### Step 3: Configure the Persona

#### Required Fields

| Field | Required | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| **Label** | Yes | A name for the persona (e.g., "Rural Farmer - Karnataka") |
| **Characteristics** | Yes | Detailed description of WHO the persona is and HOW they behave |

#### Characteristics Guidelines

Define two key aspects:

1. **WHO**: Specific details about the persona
   - Name, age, occupation
   - Location, background
   - Language proficiency

2. **HOW**: Behavioral traits
   - Communication style (polite, impatient, formal)
   - Speech patterns (slow, uses filler words)
   - Temperament (calm, easily frustrated)

**Example Characteristics:**

```text
You are Rajesh, a 45-year-old farmer from rural Karnataka who 
speaks primarily in Kannada with limited English proficiency. 

You are polite and friendly but speak slowly with natural 
pauses like "uhh" and "umm". You have a calm and patient 
demeanor but can become frustrated when technical jargon is used. 

You prefer simple, straightforward explanations and often ask 
for clarification. You tend to repeat important information 
to make sure you understood correctly.
```

<Note>
Avoid including task instructions in characteristics. Use **Scenarios** to define WHAT the persona should do.
</Note>

### Step 4: Configure Voice Settings

#### Gender

Select the voice gender for the simulated user:

| Option | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Male** | Male voice synthesis |
| **Female** | Female voice synthesis |

#### Language

Select the primary language for the persona:

| Language | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **English** | English voice and speech patterns |
| **Hindi** | Hindi voice and speech patterns |
| **Kannada** | Kannada voice and speech patterns |

#### Interruption Sensitivity

Real users often interrupt agents mid-sentence. Configure how likely this persona is to interrupt:

| Level | Behavior |
|-------|----------|
| **None** | Persona never interrupts |
| **Low** | 25% chance of interrupting |
| **Medium** | 50% chance of interrupting |
| **High** | 80% chance of interrupting |

### Step 5: Save the Persona

Click **Add persona** to create the persona.

## Updating a Persona

### Step 1: Open the Persona

1. Navigate to **Personas** from the sidebar
2. Click on the persona you want to edit
3. The edit sidebar will open with the current configuration

### Step 2: Modify Configuration

Update any of the following:

- **Label**: Change the persona name
- **Characteristics**: Update the persona description
- **Gender**: Change voice gender
- **Language**: Change primary language
- **Interruption sensitivity**: Adjust interruption behavior

### Step 3: Save Changes

Click **Save** to apply your changes.

---

## Deleting a Persona

<Warning>
Deleting a persona will remove it from any simulations that reference it. This action cannot be undone.
</Warning>

### From the Personas List

1. Navigate to **Personas**
2. Find the persona you want to delete

## Persona Fields Reference

### Complete Field List

| Field | Required | Type | Description |
|-------|----------|------|-------------|
| **Label** | Yes | string | Display name for the persona |
| **Characteristics** | Yes | string | WHO the persona is and HOW they behave |
| **Gender** | No | male/female | Voice gender (default: male) |
| **Language** | No | string | Primary language (default: english) |
| **Interruption Sensitivity** | No | none/low/medium/high | Likelihood of interrupting (default: medium) |

## Example Personas

### Rural Farmer

```text
Label: Rural Farmer - Karnataka

Characteristics:
You are Rajesh, a 45-year-old farmer from rural Karnataka who 
speaks primarily in Kannada with limited English proficiency. 
You are polite and friendly but speak slowly with natural pauses 
like "uhh" and "umm". You have a calm and patient demeanor but 
can become frustrated when technical jargon is used. You prefer 
simple, straightforward explanations.

Gender: Male
Language: Kannada
Interruption: Medium
```

### Busy Professional

```text
Label: Busy Professional - Mumbai

Characteristics:
You are Priya, a 32-year-old marketing manager in Mumbai. You 
speak fluent English and Hindi. You are direct and efficient, 
preferring quick answers over long explanations. You value your 
time and may express impatience if the conversation goes in 
circles. You speak quickly and expect the agent to keep up.

Gender: Female
Language: English
Interruption: High
```

### Elderly Customer

```text
Label: Elderly Customer - Delhi

Characteristics:
You are Sharma ji, a 68-year-old retired government employee 
from Delhi. You speak Hindi primarily with some English words 
mixed in. You are very polite and formal, often using respectful 
terms. You speak slowly and may ask the agent to repeat things. 
You appreciate patience and detailed explanations.

Gender: Male
Language: Hindi
Interruption: None
```

### Tech-Savvy Student

```text
Label: Tech-Savvy Student - Bangalore

Characteristics:
You are Arjun, a 22-year-old engineering student from Bangalore. 
You speak fluent English and use technical terminology comfortably. 
You are informal and casual in your communication style. You get 
impatient with slow responses and may interrupt to speed things up. 
You prefer self-service options when available.

Gender: Male
Language: English
Interruption: High
```

## Best Practices

### Creating Realistic Personas

1. **Be specific**: Include concrete details (name, age, occupation)
2. **Define communication style**: How do they speak? Fast, slow, formal, casual?
3. **Include limitations**: Language proficiency, technical knowledge
4. **Add emotional traits**: Patient, impatient, friendly, formal

### Personas vs Scenarios

| Aspect | Persona | Scenario |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Focus | WHO and HOW | WHAT to do |
| Content | Demographics, behavior | Task, goal, situation |
| Example | "A 45-year-old farmer who speaks slowly" | "Call to inquire about crop insurance" |

### Testing Coverage

Create personas that cover:
- Different demographics (age, occupation, location)
- Language proficiency levels
- Communication styles
- Patience levels (interruption sensitivity)
